System Overview

Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) is more than a mere metering system; it's a comprehensive energy business intelligence platform. It's the tool you need to optimize your electricity distribution, regardless of the scale of your operations.

AMI stands for "Advanced Metering Infrastructure," and it works with various meter data types, including gas, electricity, and water.

what to know

core technology

Perform real-time data analytics, giving you immediate visibility into consumption patterns and any anomalies.

Encryption and secure protocols to protect data from unauthorized access or interception.

Transmit data in real-time to the central server, ensuring that you have up-to-the-minute information.

Support bidirectional communication, enabling you to remotely manage meters, send commands, and receive alerts.

System Function Features

  • Access meter data remotely from anywhere, enabling you to monitor consumption patterns with ease.
  • Receive real-time alerts whenever power outages occur, allowing for swift response and minimal disruption to operations.
  • Optimize power distribution by balancing loads across different areas or entities, ensuring an even and efficient energy distribution.
  • Identify and mitigate electricity theft, safeguarding your revenue and protecting your bottom line.
  • Create customized reports tailored to your specific needs, track revenue, consumption, and other key metrics that matter most to your business.
  • Gain deep insights into electricity consumption patterns, allowing you to make informed decisions and further improve efficiency.

at a glance

Intelligent Application
Layer

This layer uses workflow, self-defined reports, GIS integration technology and data mining technology to provide insights to the data and provide a smart decision support system for utilities.

Smart Acquisition
Layer

This layer supports multiple protocols DLMS COSEM, IDIS, Q/GDW 376.1 through the plug-in protocol library, and is seamlessly compatible with multiple databases (Oracle, MySQL), which supports cluster deployment

Robust Communications
Layer

The communication layer supports a variety of communication methods (LAN communication: RS485, Wi-Fine, PLC, LoRa; WAN communication: GPRS, NB-IoT, Ethernet, WiFi), which can adapt to different applications

Reliable System

After fusing the several layers, Olivia delivers excellent meter reading management, power distribution management, and enterprise power consumption through power analysis model, power supply quality, load analysis, power supply reliability, power tampering analysis, power consumption analysis, and statistical reports.
